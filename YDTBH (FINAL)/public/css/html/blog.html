Emotional Wellbeing

Mental health is the health of our mind and emotions just like physical health is the health of our bodies. Unless we pay attention to our mental health, we won’t be able to achieve wholesome wellbeing. At Praan, we urge you to think about your mental health, pay attention to your emotions and take control of your wellbeing. All you need to do is remember, 'You matter because you breathe.' Let’s be aware of it's importance, get talking about it and seek help and strengthen our emotional immunity"

Depresssion

Feelings of sadness, feeling low, having a loss of interest or pleasure in daily activities is something we all experience at some point in our life. It is when these emotions persist and affect our life substantially, it may be a sign of depression. —












Am I Depressed?






Feeling low, sad and despairing
Anxiety about the future, hopelessness
Crying spells and tearfulness
Losing interest in activities you enjoyed before; sex, hobbies, etc
Feelings of worthlessness, fixating on past failures or self-blame
Sleep disturbances, insomnia or sleeping too much
Changes in appetite and weight












How can I help myself or a loved one?

  • Learn about depression; its signs and treatment options
  • Seek treatment; help look for counselors/therapists/psychiatrists, make the appointments, offer to accompany them
  • Watch out for Suicidal ideation: "I'm going to kill myself," "I wish I were dead," or "I wish I hadn't been born"
  • Support them; help them stick with the treatment - keeping appointments, medication, be willing to listen, help with chores, make plans together
  • Lastly, take care of yourself! Caring for a loved one with depression can be difficult

Anxiety

Anxiety is a common response we have to any kind of stress or threat around us. While emotions like anxiety and fear are important for motivation, the intensity at which one experiences anxiety determines if it is normal anxiety or chronic anxiety. —












Do I have Anxiety?






Feeling nervous, restless or tense
A sense sof impending danger, panic or doom
Having an increased heart rate, rapid breathing
Sweating, trembling, weakness or fatigue
Panic attacks; heart palpitations, hyperventilation, sweating, trembling, hot or cold flashes, dizziness, nausea
Sleep disturbances, including insomnia
Experiencing gastrointestinal (GI) problems
Difficulty controlling thoughts related to the trigger for anxiety











How can we self-soothe through anxiety?

  • Take long deep breaths
  • Hug a pillow/yourself - this soothes the nervous system
  • Build yourself a soothing playlist - all your favourite music
  • Release bodily energy through crying, screaming, punching a pillow, exercising, dancing, stretching
  • Help yourself understand what is going on inside through journalling or mind mapping - seeing it on paper can make it more objective
  • Find light-hearted activities you love - a funny movie or a creative hobby

Stress

Stress is our reaction to any change that requires an adjustment or response - both our body and mind are part of the reaction. Stress is a normal part of life; it can be positive - keeping us alert and motivated. But excess stress causes wear and tear - physically and emotionally. —












Am I stressed?






Cold sweating
Uneasiness in the gut - upset stomach, indigestion, reflux
Racing heart
Fast and irregular breathing
Tiredness and exhaustion
Shivering, trembling, shaking
Disturbances in sleeping and eating patterns
Disturbances in sexual activity












How can I self-soothe through Stress?

  • Take a minute to become conscious of your breathing - pay attention till it slows down and becomes even
  • Take a bath - temperature of preference, the cascading water soothes your nervous system
  • Make a playlist of soothing music and listen to it when you are feeling stressed
  • Explore guided relaxation techniques online and go through the process to relax
  • Eat a bowl of your favourite food mindfully - tasting every bite
  • Hug yourself or a pillow gently - the pressure releases happy hormones and reduces stress

Suicide

Suicide is a heavy word - the decision that end in one taking their own life. Research shows that it can be prevented very often. The key is to know the indicators and seek help. If you are feeling suicidal or know someone who is suicidal, go through the information below: —












How to identify signs of suicidal intent?






Talking about suicide — for example, making statements such as "I'm going to kill myself," "I wish I were dead" or "I wish I hadn't been born"
Withdrawing from social contact and wanting to be left alone
Having mood swings; frequent highs and lows
Feeling trapped or hopeless about a situation
Engaging in risky or self-destructive behaviours - driving fast, drinking excessively
Giving away belongings or getting affairs in order when there's no other logical explanation for doing this
Saying goodbye to people as if they won't be seen again
Developing personality changes or being severely anxious or agitated, particularly when experiencing some of the warning signs listed above
